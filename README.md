# CIVE202_Spring2026_Trans-02-01_Project-5

## Project Overview 

This project was completed for the course Civil Engineering Analysis II at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. 
The objective of the project is to better understand travel behavior for future planning and policy development. By conducting a cross-level analysis of the National Household Travel Survey (NHTS) dataset. The coalition of Midwestern Departments of Transportation tasked us with exploring travel behavior, specifically in the Midwest region and formulating at least two research questions of our own, related to travel behavior but with the condition that it must use variables that span across multiple datasets within the NHTS survey. To complete this project the coalition of Midwestern Departments of Transportation has also asked us to deliver not only quantitative analysis for both questions, but that it also be supported by visualizations and summary tables. 


The project involves:
- The development three different quantitative comparisons of the behavior of different transportation agents. Focused on localized interactions or behavior patterns.
- The cleaning and organizing of the NGSIM Foggy Bottom 200sec data.
- Completion of a quantitative analysis in python for chosen comparisons for all three transportation agents. With visualization of agent movement at the intersection using a static plot and an animation.*maybe might only do a static plot 
- Interpretation of code results or findings. Support of discussion with appropriate figures, plots, or animations. 
  
The final output includes a scope of work (SOW), a formal document defining the project goals and tasks in detail. A gantt chart providing a visual project schedule. For python code, a jupyter notebook documenting the quantitative analysis for all comparisons of agents and their supporting visualizations. Fully operational and executable without errors. As well as an annotated code document (ACD) explaning said python code, fucntions, and equations. Lastly, a written techinical report, including background on USDOT project, explanation of methods, and results.
