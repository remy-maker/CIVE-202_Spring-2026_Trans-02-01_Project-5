# CIVE202_Spring2026_Trans-02-01_Project-5

## Project Overview 

This project was completed for the course Civil Engineering Analysis II at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. 
The objective of the project is to better understand travel behavior for future planning and policy development. By conducting a cross-level analysis of the National Household Travel Survey (NHTS) dataset. The coalition of Midwestern Departments of Transportation tasked us with exploring travel behavior, specifically in the Midwest region and formulating at least two research questions of our own, related to travel behavior but with the condition that it must use variables that span across multiple datasets within the NHTS survey. To complete this project the coalition of Midwestern Departments of Transportation has also asked us to deliver not only quantitative analysis for both questions, but that it also be supported by visualizations and summary tables. 


The project involves:
- The development of two questions exploring travel behavior using variables that span accross multiple datasets within the NHTS Survey.
- The cleaning, organizing, and combination of NHTS Data including household level and vehicle level.
- Completion of a quantitative analysis in python for both proposed questions with supporting visualizations and summary tables for each question. Commenting any patterns observed.
- Interpretation of code results. Support your discussion with appropriate figure, plots, summary, statics, and thoughtful interpretation of the findings.
  
The final output includes a scope of work (SOW), a formal document defining the tasks our group unertook to develop our analysis and development of both of our travel related questions. A gantt chart providing a visual project schedule. For python code, a jupyter notebook documenting the quantitative analysis for both questions and their supporting visualizations and summary tables. Fully operational and executable without errors. As well as an annotated code document (ACD) explaning said python code, fucntions, and equations. Lastly, a written techinical report, including background on NHTS project, methods, results, and refrences.
